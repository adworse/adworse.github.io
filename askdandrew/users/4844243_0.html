    Too many sellers wait for the Greater Fool to come along and buy their properties at--or above--the asking price.     The only problem with this is that real estate is a lot of work to operate, oversee, and maintain. It's success depends on 4 major things (1) it's basis, (2) it's financing, (3) it's management, and (4) it's cost at disposition. Because of these facts, the risk profile of real estate--as an asset class--is higher; a higher risk profile demands a higher return as compensation. In multifamily, an internal rate of return that falls below 14 or 15% before taxes is unreasonable unless you are reducing it to accommodate an equity partners' required rate of return.
