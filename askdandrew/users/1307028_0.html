  • Bill, I have some advice for you to get more credible and confident:
    1) Get familiar with what's on the bank's books by using the FDIC site; know their exact numbers and their trends from last year to this year.
    2) Practice your pitch in your car when you're driving anywhere alone. Actually speak out loud what you want to say, e.g., "Hi, my name's Jim Genis from Opal Sea Capital. We're a private investment firm that purchases asset pools to provide relief for your balaknce sheet. I see you have $XXXM in nonperforming assets and that it's increased by $XXM since last year. Are you interested in selling any of these assets?" That could be your foot in the door.
    3) Just do it... over and over and over again. When I started calling asset managers, I made literally hundreds of calls and I had their FDIC info right in front of me. After the 20th call, the pitch became very smooth and I could improvise a bit.

    Hope this helps.
