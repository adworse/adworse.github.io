    Good question, Eric

    The main reason is that *all discretionary capital* is expensive.

    We're actually very competitive. However, you'll see that folks who are what we affectionately call the "411s" - meaning they just want information about financing products - have little clue as to what is going on in the capital markets.

    For example: a lot of ignorant borrower will say "I can get an FHA loan at 4%" or "my bank will lend me at 5.5%".

    The truth of the matter is: no they won't.

    Ask AJ about his dealings with the FHA. And that's for stabilized deals.

    We've had many, many intermediaries face off with borrowers who initially said "I'll go to my bank, or the FHA". The Intermediaries say "go ahead"!
    And guess what happens 30 days later? It usually goes like this: they paid a fee to some broker who promised them the world, 3% financing from a "middle-eastern bank not wanting to show their identity"... only to have come back to a Dandrew Intermediary with their tail between their legs saying "you were right".

    Neither the FHA nor a bank will lend for value-added development deals. They aren't lending at all right now. This huge settlement with JPMorganChase is going to stunt lending. Banks won't want to lend if they have to assume such liability.

    So, to circle back, it has to do with the expectations of the borrower and what they believe to be happening in the market. Not for our intermediaries.

    Pedaling capital and sex are usually the easiest things to sell. But in both cases, the buyer needs to know what they are going to do with it...

    Sal
