  • staff

    Kylie

    Are you buying this at a low basis? Or are you just buying it?

    Any involvement that you have in this deal as far as raising capital will provide enough of a value proposition for you to take *at least* 10% of the promote.

    What you've explained here is the genesis behind the Oasis II fund that we're starting later next quarter (another day, another deck....).

    First, I would hurd the cats here. Who are the remaining 52% that you need to grab 3% from? What kind of insitution worth its salt wouldn't have 51%? What is the story here? Sounds ominous. Make sure you're getting your facts straight.

    Second, I would offer the 52%'ers the ability to "get some liquidity" out of their bum investment. They can't sell it for anything more than perhaps 5 cents on the dollar - if that - because their shares are essentially worthless unless you get 51% majority.

    Once you get that, then you go to the 48% institutional holder and ask them what they want, not digressing to either parties what you are doing. If the 52%'ers know you're buying 48%, then they will think goldmine. I would say you're going to beat up the insitution in a David vs. Goliath framework...

    Regarding the 52%ers, I would first approach those who have little stakes that are perceived to be worth less than someone who owns 10% and may have deeper pockets. Go after the smaller guys and gals who probably need a few bucks for their shares like oxygen.

    Whatever you do, make sure you put a firm deadline for these folks to tender (sell) you their shares. No lingering. Put it in writing. Tell them by this date or they forever hold their peace.

    You're going to see a lot more of these as this is the dutch auction technique that we've built OFREEFund.com around.

    Tell me how it goes. This isn't rent money but could be a good windfall if you've got the patience and stamina.
