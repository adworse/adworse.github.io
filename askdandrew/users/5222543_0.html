  • Sal,
    I too am obviously not an attorney, however in most states from what I've read, unless your arranging residential mortgages, you do not need to be licensed or bonded.

    As Intermediaries, we are not acting in the capacity of a broker. A mortgage Broker in the traditional sense of the title is someone who takes in a loan request, packages the loan for underwriting purposes and submits that package through a internal network of funding sources and waits for a quote and a good faith estimate of costs.

    We are dealing with private institutional monies from one resource, meaning Dandrew, so there is a big difference between what we do and what those who act in the traditional capacity of a Mortgage Broker does.

    I have never had a real estate broker, a commercial client or anyone else in a transaction ask for a brokers license.
