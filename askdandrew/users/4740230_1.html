Sal,
I had to jump off the call today and get onto a call for the medical project but wanted to comment on the guys deal he went into with you and AJ. This is for those looking at their first deal.

Sal has taught me to look more at the inner workings of the deal outside of the players to get at the real story of the deal and not what the country music love song highlights.

I look at deals daily with assumable mortgages and to the untrained eye, these look like selling points in the opportunity and they aren't selling points as much as red flags.

First, just because the loan is assumable doesn't mean it's assumable for you or your client. The holder of that loan is going to want a very strong entity coming in. They don't want to have a good loan turn bad.

Next, you have to look at how long the investor is going to be in the deal. If they are paying $15,000,000.00 for a property and the NOI is $500,000.00 or even $1,000,000.00, how long is the investor going to be in the deal at it's worse case scenario.

The next thing to look at is what are the avenues available to get out of the deal and retire the capital and debt stacks. Can they refinance? is the timing of the market optimum for selling the property, what is the viable exit strategy.

I just wanted to pass these thoughts on.
