Sal, I have been asked what and if we require a deposit to do any loans. I know there are no upfront fees but what is a good answer regarding application fee and expense deposits. Thanks and have a great Labor Day weekend.
Judy Groome commented Sep 5 2015
  • staff

    Judy,

    Great question. Every institutional lender will require a earnest deposit fee. This depends on the size of the loan but it usually used exclusively for appraisals and legal fees to paper the deal.

    We’re usually around $25,000 to $35,000.

    Under no circumstances will a lender proceed with due diligence fees without having a monetary commitment from the borrower to cover these costs.

    If your borrower balks, then he or she simply doesn’t have the money and you shouldn’t work with them.

    *Performing Notes* are priced on a yield basis. Meaning, the note is bid based off of current % and discounted further to hit a yield bogey. If its currently at a 8, and our yield bogey (or target) is say, a 15%, we'll discount the face value to a bid that gives us the 15%.

    Does this make sense?
