Thread in Marketing - Pearl

So, how successful have each of these students been in this business (except Adam, it was mentioned how many deals he has submitted) - number of deals submitted/closed? If we are to learn from these students it would be helpful to know not only how they are marketing, but what success they are having. Thanks, T.

Teri Domanski commented May 8 2013
  • staff

    This is a great question.

    To answer your question, over the past 3 years, we’ve not opened up this club to new members until recently. Like any other organization, the top 20% we have rise to the top and give us the most deal flow until they *quit on us to start their own mini-funds or syndications for their own deals.*

    Going over the deals in specificity is located on the Dandrew Partners’ Shareholders Meeting Transcript that you should have received and read already as part of your training/education.

    Regarding the deals that they submit and get closed, about 60% are DPOs from smaller community banks that have been abandoned from larger institutions.

    The FDIC and scripts we provided a year ago provided a lot of assistance to these intermediaries.
    These assets were all Four Food groups, meaning Office, Retail, Multifamily and Industrial.

    The other 40% are from folks looking to buy value added stuff.
    *No raw land*. I know you’ve maybe heard me speak to busted developments in one of our to-be-launched funds but that is not “land” per se.
    Most Alumni have started their own JVs or syndications using concepts in the Private Equiteer and other resources to take investor money and start small, but purchasing an apartment complex or smaller pool of REO assets.

    I hope this answers your question. Again, *I would urge you to check your emails for the shareholders meeting transcript as that is something sent only by email in a sequence to new members.*
