Sal, here are a few questions I have pertaining to the DIP Course they are as follows:
*What are the best ways to market other than direct mail & email to trustees and attorneys ? suggestions please.
*Are there any other types of property that would be considered other than income producing?
*Are there any other prequals other than the five data points, entity doc, copy of bk filing, property financials, debt stack details, use & poa once out of bk ?
*What is the customary fee paid to the intermediary in a “DIP” transaction ?
*What is a realistic timeframe for a “DIP” transaction to close from the time of submission?
*Are there very many students pursuing this area of your programs ?
*Are you a ware of any large players in this arena ?

Thank you, I look forward to your response.

Fernando

PS. I would have asked Devin these questions but I havent herd from Devin as per your email from 8-22 ?

Fernando Muriel commented Sep 1 2012
  • staff

    Fernando. Great questions and I'll answer them here:

    *What are the best ways to market other than direct mail & email to trustees and attorneys ? suggestions please.*
    The students that are killing it are using LinkedIn. Period. That's all.

    *Are there any other prequals other than the five data points, entity doc, copy of bk filing, property financials, debt stack details, use & poa once out of bk ?*
    No. That's it.

    *What is the customary fee paid to the intermediary in a “DIP” transaction ?*
    1 point of amount financed plus 50% share of the equity kicker that goes to Dandrew.

    *What is a realistic timeframe for a “DIP” transaction to close from the time of submission? *
    I've seen these close inside of 7 days and some take 4 months. Its all about the motivation of the trustee and the parties involved.

    *Are there very many students pursuing this area of your programs?*
    Not really... Some think its too complicated. The smart ones get it and do it.

    *Are you a ware of any large players in this arena?*
    Yes. Anything over $30mm *invested* will have a lot of players. We're mid market guys and that is the market that is overlooked by the large players.
