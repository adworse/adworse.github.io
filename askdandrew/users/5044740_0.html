County taxing authorities don't require sq ft in the tax records so I have no idea what the building sq ft is to determine a possible lease amount. 2014-15 tax records set a value of $2,957,941 for land and structure. Taxes paid this tax year $32,049.

Although this is a small project the upside is a NPA, in foreclosure the bank needs to unload in this last fiscal quarter. We also have a potential tenant and future buyer as part of exit strategy. Lastly, by solving the bank's problem I can probably get access to the bank's distressed asset portfolio and its circle of influence with other regional banks.

Sal,
Followed up on the commercial real estate deal we recently discussed and learned today that the building is in escrow. I'm not upset because I learned some valuable lessons:
1. Never judge the book by its cover - this was not a $500k deal it was a $42 mil deal. The charter school had a bigger project due to having several other sites that needed improvement/renovation. Didn't know this. That's why the financing fell through the first time. School was going to float a bond but some private investors checked out the property, finally understood what the school wanted, and agreed to provide financing for the deal.
2. Had I had the consulting course, which I'm presently taking, I could have put a pitch book together to grease the wheels. The school didn't know how to pitch prospective investors to fund the project. I could have gotten a piece of the action.
3. My instincts were correct regarding going after the project. The intellectual skill set I've developed by taking Dandrew classes allowed me to spot this opportunity and follow through. I didn't get this one but there are plenty other opportunities. I will find them.
