    Keith:

    3rd Party Stress here means that the loan that was orignated couldn't be sold off in the secondary market.

    There are (were) two types of lenders: moving companies and storage companies. During the credit boom, many lenders would underwrite these loans and sell them off immediately to pension funds, life companies, etc.

    So these would be called "scratch and dent" as they couldn't get sold for one reason or another.
