    Dennis:

    Retail investors are – as a rule – a huge pain in the ass. They don’t understand markets, deals and like to pretend they are the big man/woman on campus.

    If they have a business plan, which during this preliminary point all I would need to see is their:

    1. *Bios.*
    Who they are and what they do. Resumes, etc.

    2. *Deal Sheet.*
    A list of deals they’ve done. Includes: deal name, asset type, basis they bought at, basis they exited out, return to investors;

    Then that would be enough to see if they qualify for a JV Equity Facility. Meaning they have a checkbook that requires 2 signatures (ours and their’s…)

    Review your Private Equiteer training (JV vs Fund Structures) for more info on this.

    Dennis

    I apologize that I’m getting to this now as I have been traveling lots lately, expecially late in April.

    I would tell them that this is a *very specialized structured product* and this is expensive, both for your fees and the cost of capital.

    Start there. People who want equity facilities (“lines of equity”) will pay on a typical profit split promote and an IRR look back of around 10-12% depending on how much new and fresh equity they are bringing to the table.

    If you don’t know what this means, this is what JV Equity vs Fund Structures is about in your Private Equiteer program.
