Thread in Bulk REO Financier™

Hey guys I wanted to post something I mentioned in the last conference call, it was about these so called NPN, REO pool brokers.

My thing is.... KEEP AWAY FROM THEM. Ask me how I know that.

I have wasted a huge amount of time screwing around with these guys who say they have access to these tapes. The only thing worse than wasting money is time because you can get money back but not time. If I actually tried to get in touch with more banks then I'd have put in a good deal and got paid from Dandrew by now.

I understand the feeling of wanting to work with these Brokers. They already have a Relationship that you want to establish with banks, so what’s the problem? Well they will most likely ask for way more points than Dandrew wants to pay out for once.

I’ve talked with enough of these clowns to know how to qualify them quickly.

1) Ask them how both of us (you and the broker) are going to get paid?
2) If they say it’s 3 points (which they think is industry standard but way to aggressive) tell them that’s not going to work because at the most you’ll get paid out (correct me if I’m wrong Sal and crew) up to 1.5 points. BUT tell them you’d be more than happy to split the profits with them 50/50 because (to use one of my own lines) you’d rather get 50% of something then 100% of nothing.
3) Extra credit bonus: If they do get irritated since they’re not going to get the big rock star points. Tell them what you can’t do with points you can make up with on volume.
4) A massive WARNING SIGN to these brokers who aren’t serious. They will blow the heck up out of your phone. This is a massive warning sign that they need you WAY MORE than you need them.


Now most will say “Ooga booga booga I’m an investor rock star,” (paraphrased of course) and other stupid crap like that.

Personally the last clueless guy made himself out to say he met a corportate officer at a big bank through networking. When I was send the contract he send me an “irrevocable master fee agreement,” which screams BULLSH*T. Plus like I said with #4 him and one of his partners were calling 2-3 times a day so that was 6 phone calls to my cell.

When I did talk to the original guy I told him I couldn’t agree to that since it wasn’t my money, but what I could agree to is splitting my money 50/50. He got upset. I told him, “I’m an investor just like you.” He said, “You’re not an investor like me.” I laughed and thought that I guessed he wasn’t like me because he doesn’t want to get paid.

There were others I’ve come across who were more relaxed about it. They agreed with my philosophy of 50% of something vs. 100% of nothing. Their deals didn’t look like I could submit to Dandrew without getting yelled at by Sal but still those folks are out there.

Hope this helps with Dealing with REO and Note Brokers.

    Dana: You are wrong! What is happeneing to my MasterClass? Pref Equity and Mezz are 2 different structured products in the captial structure. Like a tire and a hubcap. Similar, but not really.

    Mezz is secured by the assignment of partnership interests in the LLC so in the event of default, the mezz holder can take over the partnership interests (or shares of stock) in the LLC that holds the asset.

    *Be sure to review this page from the Dandrew Triage Capital October Manual.* I put a lot of work into putting that illustration together.

    Lastly, what is on the tape and how it is defined is strictly a function of what the bank wants to put on there. There is no set rule and because I'm cynical at best and paranoid at worst, I would suggest that you ask. Never, never create your own tape because that could cause a lot of problems if we bid on something that we think is a 1st but is really a mezz piece. I don't want you placed into the crossfire!
