Thread in Ask The Dandrew Team

Sal,

Good to see you back at the helm. I wanted to pass on a strategy I have used for years to build a book of contacts in the commercial real estate space.

I go to the website www.cityfeet.com and I type in the city and state as well as select the asset class I want to search. I then look at each property advertised for sale. I then wrote down the brokers agency name, brokers name, phone number and email address if listed.

I then call that broker during day time business hours, which is very important to know, because you look much more professional when you call during business hours.

I introduce myself as follow. My name is James Balter, I am the CEO and Director of Capital Markets for JJB Capital Advisors. I am calling you today because I was once referred to you as someone who has clients who needc funding for commercial real estate opportunities.

When can we meet at your office to discuss doing some business. That is it, From there, the response is usually either, when are you available James, right now I do not have any clients who need funding but do you have a media package for your firm you can email me or I have a client or clients who really need funding and then its either lets meet today or tomorrow or I get to hear a country music song.

Either way, this is a system I have used for years and years, from my days of brokering notes in the early 1990's to today. Try it, it works.
