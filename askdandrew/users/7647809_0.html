3. You said in one of the videos on AskDandrew.com, that the bank holding the REO will put new financing on a REO up to 80% and Dandrew may consider 20% as a second. My question is would the bank which is holding the REO that is providing the new financing be considered the sponsor/owner as well as the primary lender in this case?

4. Give examples of cases where properties have no sponsor? AJ didn't go into details about this. I can only think of auctions.

Kenneth Minors commented Dec 14 2015
  • staff

    Kenneth. Your wish is my command… This is like a test.

    1. Yes and no. Foreclosure only transfers ownership. It doesn’t mean an eviction will have taken place or there is an intent to evict. The new owner, in the case the bank, will want to keep the tenants in place and put a management company in place.

    2. NDAs and Confidentiality Agreements can be considered one and of the same. In the bulk reo universe, brokers want you to sign NDAs as they are fools and don’t know what they are doing. You won’t be dealing with them because we won’t look at anything from a broker and you know that. Confidentiality agreements, on the other hand, usually come from banks who are looking to protect their borrower’s sensitive information when sending tapes of NPLs over to us to bid on.

    3. (“You said”. I love these…) The bank is never going to want to be a sponsor. They will only want to be the lender. I can’t get into reasons why here as I don’t have the time – and you can easily Google those reasons – however, banks lend, not own real estate.

    4. In commercial? Anything that has been foreclosed and the bank is the owner of course. Auctions are merely just another mechanism to *sell* a property.

    Thanks
    Sal
