How does a commercial npn get valued if your dealing with the bank not the operator.
When it comes to the five data points, is this something the bank would have? Or, do
we use some other metric...what metrics would we use?

Hurol Samuel commented Oct 8 2013
  • staff

    Hurol

    Great question. Simple answer. And you being in Brooklyn, I can only imagine what you’re looking at in that overheated market…

    We want to see *value over price*. I may want $5000 for an ounce of gold, but if its only worth $1300 or so, then that’s not a deal.

    So, the 5 Data Points is the first way we look at this if it is an income producing commercial real estate asset.

    If it is income producing, but vacant, then we’ll look at comparable trades - *meaning what similar assets have sold for, not have been asked for* - on a per door or per square foot basis.

    Now that we have an understanding, now we can prepare a bid by backing out some costs. Notably, foreclosure costs, etc.

    This is where the “story” comes into place; have the current borrower filed for BK? Has the foreclosure been filed? How much in repairs needed. This
    is where those models I gave to you come in handy…
