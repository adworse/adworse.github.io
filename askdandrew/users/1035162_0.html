    Jacqueline, this is AJ responding. Let me try to direct you here.

    "do I just try to find their contact info and send the Ex Sum? "
    Absolutely not. Under no circumstances would this work. Working with these people is all about relationships. These people want to meet you in person, listen to you speak about your area of specialty, ask you hard questions, and watch your eyes when you respond.

    You need to meet with them. How? Get yourself invited to one of their private meetings. How do you get in? Well, those who are members of the Expert Network get this level of instruction from us. Are you an Expert Network member?

    As far as your questions for the developer, this is on the other end of the spectrum from the first question you asked about building the right relationship with the capital. These developer questions need to come from the qualified sponsor on this deal, whatever it is. Is this you?

    I looked over these development questions, and they all look very good...but my biggest question is this. Will the same person who meets with the family offices face to face be the person responsible for asking these questions to the developer? Are you a fund manager where your fund is providing funding for a sponsor, or are you going to try to be both the fund manager and the sponsor?

    I would give you strong caution if you were to attempt the latter, as even we have a team of people to spread this across here at Dandrew.

    Hopefully this helps direct your thought process here.
