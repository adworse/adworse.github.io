Jeraldine Fitzgerald commented May 8 2014
  • staff

    My Dear Jeraldine,

    You’re the 5th intermediary in the past month who has time on a radio show. How come I don’t get asked to be on a radio show? Is it because of my salty language sometimes? Now I’m sad.

    *Frankly, this is a tall order.* But I’m answering these for you because I’m hoping you’ll be able to get a copy of you on the show talking about these points so the rest of the Dandrew Nation can cheer you on. Deal?

    1. A history of real estate investing’s part in lifting our nation out of recessions.
    Frankly, I could write a doctoral dissertation on this subject alone. However, follow my drift below in “Bold*:

    *Housing has always been a wealth creator for Americans throughout the history of this country have been able help our nation in several different ways. Primarily, investors have been the mechanism for creating an efficient market when the housing markets have traditionally have been inefficient. They provide pricing discovery at the time they invest and resell to owner occupants who may not ordinarily qualify for traditional funding. Real estate investors think outside of the box to provide meaningful solutions to a majority of the population. This allows the average American to create wealth meaningfully.*

    2. How RE investors can benefit the average American.
    *Real estate investors benefit the average American by allowing them to get into opportunities for home ownership that frankly isn’t available to a majority of the population today. Banks and lenders are only lending to the top 10% of the population who have strong credit. Unfortunately, this leave most of the working class boxed out.
    Real estate investors can also help get those homeowners who may be upside down out of their situations by doing the necessary negotiations with that homeowner’s lender. Investors are liquid, have readily available access to capital and this is one of the ways in which investors make markets efficient for both the buyer and the seller. It’s the ability to meaningfully craft the solution and do the necessary heavy lifting that benefits both buyers and sellers.*

    3. Bridge loans and their part in today’s economy.
    *Private capital today creates the mechanism for transactions to move. It’s the lubrication in the market that allows a buyer and seller to come together to create a market. When there is no access to capital, this is what causes housing prices to go down.
    Bridge capital has its place as it allows investors to access capital for deals and opportunities that can’t be financed via traditional methods such as banks. This is why private investors are so important to the overall real estate market.*

    Let me know if this helps and send us the recording as soon as you can get your hands on it.

    Sal
