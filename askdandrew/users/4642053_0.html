    Faith.

    I see your new. Welcome! From a Fund Managers perspective, I'm looking at this different than that of an operator.

    So, let me tell you what this would look like if I brought this to our Investment Committee. I, too, am in the business and have a great track record of reducing risks and throwing good, steady-Eddie returns to our LPs. So, let me put my cynical hat on and continue:

    I don't understand this deal. What I hear is how *you* want to make money, and how *you* want to structure the deal, how much *you* want to make in consulting fees, a 10% brokerage fee to *you*, running it through *your* Roth (you must mean self directed IRA) but I have no idea about the basis (what you are getting in at), who the Sponsor is going to be, do they have "green" experience (many say they do and don't) and *how I make money*. What is a peer investor? Who are they? What is their experience? What are they bringing to the table. is that what you mean by "skin"? Skin means someone is contributing cash or assets to a deal.

    California development? The California Coastal Commission is scary. Has your Sponsor dealt with them before?

    Long story short, I don't think this deal is for us. But I want you to see how this deal is thought of through the eyes of an investor, meaning capital partner.

    The development deals we're doing now are not ground up eco deals in what many in this industry call a "vanity industry", but rather a broken development that needs capital to finish it up.

    One example we're working on is a Walgreens that the developer couldn't finish and has a 40 year lease ready to go when he has it finished.

    That is what we're doing.

    Keep up the great work! Sorry if I was a little harsh, but this is how any PE firm like ours would analyze this.
