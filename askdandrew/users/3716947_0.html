    Emisano: Great questions.

    First you’re going to want to use the infographic blueprints that we drop to you on a continual basis as a member. When you got started, you should have received an Owner’s Manual and an infographic called the “Pyramid of Priority”. This will tell you who to contact and in what priority.

    Try to stay away from marketing directly to owners. The reason being is that the Property Managers – as per our Intermediaries’ experience - is much more effective as some of them *actually drive the decision-making* (wealthy owners will just tell them to “handle it” for them).

    Also, in the Private Banker Identity Kit (http://www.PrivateBankerIdentityKit.com) there are 2 great blueprints that show you how to use VA’s or what we call Acquisition Specialists to help you bird dog for these deals. It shows you how to compensate them, hire them, and manage them easily. They don’t need to be in you office, as many of our I’maries’ use virtual assistants to do this.

    Also, I’m releasing an infographic soon for free that will focus on which *Groups on LinkedIn* you should be using. I’m still waiting for my friends on Wall Street to get back to me with their favorite groups to use.

    Title companies can be a great source too. They are on the “first responders” when a deal doesn’t close due to funding.

    Lastly, remember your *Elevator Pitch: “… our company is a capital provider and we fund a lot of things. Our clients are folks who have found something interesting to buy but don’t have all the capital they need, or are folks who have been offered the opportunity to buy their note back at a discount. Those are our main client profiles. “*

    Remember, the best experience you can get is simply by *submitting deals to us*.

    *You need to rest assured that if you find something interesting, we’re here to help you.”
