  • staff

    Leave it to AH to ask a question of this complexity... Its not a difficult answer, just one that requires a little more digging deeper relative to advantagious tax strategies and structuring away the rist for the LPs (limited partners or investors in our funds and other funds like Dandrew).

    Please note before I give my response that I am not an accountant and am not authorized to render legal or accounting advice.

    We’re very, very tax sensitive. We understand that most of our investors (LPs) pay taxes. We understand that there’s a difference between ordinary income and capital gains. So we’re using what I think are fairly creative structures which you’ll see as we go through these assets. We’re structuring preferred returns. We’re structuring something as preferred equity which we could have chose to structure it as first trust debt, but we’re using unleveraged preferred equity which is really a proxy for first trust debt and now giving the LPs cash flow which is return of capital, not return on capital, and we’re giving our LPs cash flow that you will have tax deferment on for two or three years.
