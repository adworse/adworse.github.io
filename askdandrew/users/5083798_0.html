    Molly

    This is the "gray area" of real estate. You need to accept that there isn't a hard-and-fast rule.

    Think of this as a slice of pie:

    First, there is usually a preferred return (pref) that is split out first to the *investor* (us).

    Then there is a pre-determined split. This is when the beans are split up and investor usually gets a majority of that split. Why? Because we bear a lot of economic risk. Pure and simple.

    So this is usually, in industry jargon explained as a "*Y after a X*".

    Deals today usually are a 70 after a 10. Or 70% promote to the investor after a 10% pref.
