Hi Sal, I'm looking at a 260 unit multifamily in Las Vegas that has an conduit loan with UPB of $10.5M. The pro forma value is $13.9M. The Broker says the seller will accept an assumption. The property is 32% vacant though, and is running at a slight negative after expenses and debt service. Also, there are only 2 years before the 10 year balloon comes due (in 2016). There is the defeasance to consider as well, which would increase the payoff about $1M.

I was thinking of offering just an assumption, no equity to the Seller with the Seller paying all closing costs, which it sounds like he would probably take. I would be assigning the contract if he does accept. With the vacancy, the negative, and the loan coming due in a couple of years though, would this property be marketable as it is? Is there a better way to approach it, or just pass?
