Thread in Conference Call 09-28-2012

Another call that was a home run! Sal, you gave us a PDF from Stifel Nicolaus that had previous pricing expectations in Q3. I understood everything but couldn't figure out what "comparable TBAs stands for. Also, are jumbo hybrids used on higher valued homes? Lastly, does this info reflect RBMS loans? Are they the FNMA/FHLMC securities that are referred to?

Jerome Wilkerson commented Sep 28 2012
  • staff

    Jerome.
    Answers in the orders that you asked:

    *TBA Trades*:
    A term used to describe a forward mortgage-backed securities trade. Essentially, the term TBA market is derived from the fact that the actual mortgage-backed security that will be delivered to fulfill a TBA trade is not designated at the time the trade is made. The securities are "to be announced" 48 hours prior to the established trade settlement date.

    Similar to pre-ordering many pizzas. You know what you want in the order and you pay ahead a day or so.

    *Jumbo-Hybrids*:
    Yes, bigger, higher valued homes.

    Yes this all affects RMBS loans.

    *However, this is something that you don't really need to worry about as it relates to these questions because we/you deal in the "whole loan secondary mortgage market" and not RMBS*.

What does UBPI stand for and what makes you avoid it? Does this mean that we won't be able to approach RIA's? Good info on the housing price range and strategy.

Jerome Wilkerson commented Jun 6 2013
  • staff

    Jerome:

    An IRA is most likely to be taxed when it earns unrelated business taxable income (UBTI). The UBTI rules apply to all qualified retirement plans, not just traditional IRAs. If an IRA earns UBTI exceeding $1,000 it must pay income taxes on that income. The IRA might have to file Forms 990-T or 990-W. It also must pay estimated income taxes during the year if the UBTI exceeds $500.

    The IRA owner essentially will be taxed twice on UBTI. The IRA will be taxed on the income. Subsequently, the owner or beneficiary will be taxed on distributions of that income. There is no deduction or credit available to the owner for UBTI paid by the IRA, and the tax on the IRA does not increase the tax basis of the IRA.

    So, there you go. Taxes.
