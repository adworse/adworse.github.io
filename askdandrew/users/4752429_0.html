Thread in Conference Call 02-07-2014

LinkedIn is a great lead generator. There is a law concerning working with foreign investors:

"In 1980, the U.S. Congress passed the Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act, more commonly known by the acronym FIRPTA, to tax foreigners' gains on income from and sale of U.S. real estate and other real property. Before FIRPTA became law, the U.S. had no way to tax foreigners on these profits. Now, FIRPTA requires withholding from a foreigner's rental income as well as withholding from a foreigner's gains (or losses) upon disposal of U.S real estate." (Huffington Post, 2013)

Source article: What Is FIRPTA, and How Does It Affect My Commercial Real Estate Investment?

http://www.huffingtonpost.com/phil-jemmett/what-is-firpta_b_3982816.html

  • Suzette,

    According to the IRS code, http://www.irs.gov/Individuals/International-Taxpayers/FIRPTA-Withholding
    "other than solely as a creditor, in real property" a solution may be to be a lender rather than a title holder of real property.

    And acting "solely as a creditor" the 'lender' may participate in profits upon sale of the property with a note that gives the lender a portion of the profits upon the eventual resale or refinancing. A participation loan.

    I've used the 'participation' structure in notes and mortgages/deeds of trust when I wanted to stay out of the chain of title but needed to protect my position and sale profits.

    Just a suggestion . . .
