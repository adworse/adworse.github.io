Thread in Term Sheet Review

Why is subordinate debt never permitted, so much so that you guys will continue to do frequent (monthly?) title searches checking to make sure none has appeared after the deal has funded? Why is it so important to discover new subordinate debt, and then even require it be cured, or call the loan due, if it is found? It's junior to your loan so what's the problem. (Sorry if this is dumb question but I'm just not seeing it.)

Perry T. Plotkin commented Aug 31 2013
  • staff

    Perry

    This is a very, very good question. It has to do with dealing ahead of time as to what the worst is that can happen.

    More specifically, this means if the borrower files *bankruptcy*. In the eyes of the court, anything that is recorded against the property in the form of a mortgage (or a deed of trust) could be considered by a bankruptcy court a *secured creditor*. And the second could have the same rights and the first in Bankruptcy pecking order if a judge feels that way.

    So, if this asset is placed in Bankruptcy, the senior doesn’t want to share the beans with another secured creditor as that could affect what the first mortgage holder could get back from a forced bankruptcy liquidation.

    I’ve seen where a judge could deem the second being *equal* as the first, meaning they are splitting the proceeds “Even-Steven.”

    Also, the first doesn’t want to have monies earmarked for repairs and improvements being used to pay an interest payment on a second. They want to be first. No girlfriends on the side.

    I’ll talk about this more on the continuing ed on video.

    Great question.
    Sal

  • staff

    1. Yes. We always use a newly-formed SPE.

    2. I’m not sure. Can you elaborate more?

    3. Terms such as Bankruptcy-remote is *not placed into the contract*, but rather *into the Operating Agreement* of the newly formed LLC.

    6. Yes and No. Appraisal has many different values on them. We say exactly which value we want. Its like telling your wife the difference between you coming home with a Mercedes or a C250…
