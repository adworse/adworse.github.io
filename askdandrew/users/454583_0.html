We want them to be fully trained and to be able to do marketing and find deals too? Would we give that person access to our training site? Do we let them know that we are partnering with Dandrew? What would keep them from getting the training and then going around me? Would I want to have that person sign a non-compete contract? Also, you have said we could get an intern and not pay them anything? How can that be worth it to them? Any additional information on this issue is appreciated.

Deborah Rager commented Jan 26 2011
  • staff

    Superdeb! Peter has an "Intern Program" that is due to be unveiled in the not too distant future, which will be very informative. You can really determine which tasks you would like an intern to perform based on your needs - whether it be cold calling, sending them to conferences to make new contacts or to analyze deals etc...as you so desire. The beauty of hiring interns is that as a start-up you gain a talented individual full of energy without the expense of an employee or the commitment to one. Why would they go for this? In my opinion, qualifications alone just don't cut it anymore in the marketplace. Employers value relevant experience in the real world and certainly in this economy it is not just new grads that are offering their services to companies for no salary. Few jobs + intense competition = a need to strengthen the resume. In short, internships are a useful way to gain crucial experience and they can lead to that ever important first job. My first position came through an internship... Hope that helps. James
