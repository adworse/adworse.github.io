General Question: Sal if you were forced to choose only one method in the current market, what would it be?

Hard money financing? Looking for bulk NPN & REO product? Bankruptcy?

Will Stoltzfus commented Jul 30 2013
  • staff

    Good question!

    My answer is: Neither.

    I'd want to know something about everything because in my experience being so “siloed” or highly specialized in one strategy or play is dangerous for the following reasons:

    1. *That strategy can become obsolete over time and can happen quickly.*
    For example, residential short sales. This happens in all markets, stocks and bonds too.

    2. *Will unconsciously force you to look over other opportunities as you’re focused looking at just one transaction type.*
    If someone brings you a deal that is commercial where you have the ability to make $50,000 alone in points, you’ll never know its an opportunity if you’re looking at just bulk REOs and NPLs, say.

    3. *People will respect you and send you more deal flow if they know you are a “generalist”.*
    That means, you know enough to be dangerous about several things and not just one thing. As a result, your deal flow will increase.

    4. *You can’t control the type of deal flow that comes in. Just the quantity of your sourcing*
    No matter how long you’ve been in this business, someone at some point is going to send you something that may not fit in your current “wheelhouse”, but if you have an educated eye, you’ll know how to engineer it for big profits.

    Case in point, I crossed a trade with a prominent Asian family who wanted to deploy several millions of dollars into a commodity futures fund. They didn’t know who to go to, I did and now I get annual checks of about $40,000 for that intro.

    That is why, in a long-winded answer, that I teach all of our Intermediaries to be a generalist.
