Hi Sal and Corey,
A bank will send us some distressed assets to evaluate after we sign and return a Confidentiality Agreement. The CA allows us to share information with employees, contractors, consultants and agents after we get them to sign a separate CA. Here’s the language:

“Prospective Purchaser shall carefully restrict access to the Due Diligence Information and the Confidential Information only to those of its employees, contractors, consultants, and agents (“Prospective Purchaser’s Representatives”) if and when absolutely necessary and, before any such disclosure and require those persons or entities to sign non-disclosure restrictions substantially in the form of Exhibit “A” attached hereto, prior to such
engagement, disclosure or release, as applicable. Prospective Purchaser shall promptly deliver an original signed copy of the Confidentiality Agreement to the Bank from each such third party consultant, if any, upon receipt.”

Please advise if you have any problems or concerns or if we can proceed. Thanks.

    This is fine. There is nothing wrong with this. Banks will require you to sign a Confidentiality Agreement (aka "Confi", "CA")and this is standard policy and procedure. Why? Most info on there is confidential and you wouldn't want us brandishing your financials all over the place. Right? Same with the banks borrowers on the loans you're getting.

    Sign the CA, and this warms my heart knowing you're direct with the bank.

    I will sleep well tonight.
