    Danny,

    So I really don’t understand your question. I know you’re an Arbitrager, but have you watched any of the content? This material is discussed in depth in the videos and the manual.

    But since you asked, we run assets into our proprietary Excel model. This model has algorithms that take into account several things, but not limited to time to foreclose, pricing increases or decreases over a period of time for that ZIP, etc.

    That is how we formulate an *indicative bid*.

    If the seller agrees to that range, then we place a *commitment letter*
    stating the terms are subject to full due diligence. So if the values hold, the bid stabilizes to a *final bid*.

    The final bid is prepared by either doing an AVM or drive buy 1055 appraisal BPO method. We hire a firm such as ClearCapital to run these BPOs for us – again described in the videos – and if there are no *value fades* we’ll proceed with providing an *asset level bid* to send to the seller.

    We also do title searches to make sure the seller has the *right* to sell these assets (which is one of the many, many reasons we want you to be direct with the seller) and another search for borrowers in BK.

    Some of these assets may get kicked out for whatever reason, or the bid for the individual asset may fade depending on how these reports come back.

    Then we continue to move on to the closing, usually with a pre-negotiated holdback for to make sure the *servicing has been released* properly to our servicer. Sometimes banks will play hanky-panky because they want to collect an extra months or 2 months of servicing.

    So that is the cliff notes version.

    We’ll talk about this more at the event and I know you’ll be there. Bring your deals.

    Sal
