Reta Hansen commented May 10 2013
  • staff

    Reta,
    Good questions. Below is your blueprint:

    We don’t want to give a wholesale, cross-the-board numbers; this risks your credibility.

    If you running to a bank and say we’ll buy everything at a 25% haircut, you risk looking foolish and perhaps over-promising on assets that very well might as well be worth close to nothing.

    Case in point, in the Oasis Fund, we bought a package of assets from a busted hard money lender. One of them is a piece of raw land, “lizards and snakes” territory with an irrigation system that we have no idea what to do with it. Alan says it would make a great marijuana farm but we don’t want to be in that business…

    So your tasks are as follows here:
    1. *Contact local banks to see what they have on their books by telling them that you are a capital provider with a few qualified operators looking to source deals.*
    You’ve got enough of our training to know how to hunt, what to hunt for, where to find your pray and how to skin it.

    2. *Don’t make promises. Just look at them and value those assets by the 5 Data Points.*
    Current NOI x 10 and that will give you an understanding of an approximate value.

    3. If you have the *“Land Development Financier” you’re going to have to look at recently traded similar product to gauge what these built-out parcels could be worth.*
    Do some simple Google Searches or perhaps Loopnet.com to get some info there. Don’t go crazy! Just do desktop work. No driving to look at it, you sit at home with your iPad (great for iPads) or laptop and do these.

    4. *If you find something interesting, pass it along to us via www.dandrewdeals.com and then we’ll advise you if it’s a good deal or not.*
    Be dispassionate about the deal. Don’t fall fatally attracted to it.

    5. *If it is a good deal, then you need to hunt for a qualified operator.*
    This is very easy to do.

    Very simple. But don’t ever offer numbers. You’ll regret it later.

    Sal
