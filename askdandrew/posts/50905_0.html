Hello again Sal,

Midway in the video you say "(receivers) may be appointed to the lender before the BK". If it is not yet a BK (not yet reached the court), WHO is appointing the Receiver? How can a Receiver BE appointed if it is not yet "in court"?

Michele Spitz commented May 27 2014
  • staff

    Michele,

    Great question.

    The lenders may appoint a receiver, depending on how the loan docs read. Early in any judicial foreclosure action, the lender that brought the action will often ask the court to appoint a receiver to take control of the property away from the borrower. Appointment of a receiver represents a crucial step in the foreclosure process. The receivership is also intended to assure that the property does not deteriorate under the control of a distracted and penniless borrower.

    Does this answer your question?

    Sal
