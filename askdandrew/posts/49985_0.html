Regarding Todd's deal. It was clear early on that the borrower wanted to play poker with Todd by showing his bluff relating to the single hard money offer he currently claimed he was able to execute on in two weeks.

Would it have been proper to respond as follows: I would have immediately called out the borrower upon identifying that another hard money source had given him a commitment. My response would have been: Show me your Term Sheet and if you can't, go do your shopping now, because if we issue a term sheet, you have 5 business days to accept and if you don't and choose to walk away, I can assure you that when you come back, you'll get 8 points up front and 16% interest (those extra point$ / $pread are mine). The bottom line, put up or shut up and don't waste my time. This is supply and demand 101. I certainly would be more tactful in my choice of words but the inherent message would be the same.

Also, regardless of whether this was a good deal or not, up front or during Todd's initial discussion with the borrower, while managing the borrowers expectations, a very important fact should have (any may have) been discussed that should have proven to the borrower, despite a 12-14% interest rate, that he gets to now keep $XXXXX monthly in cash sweep profits that are now being directed to the lender. By not accepting these terms he is screwing himself and or other members, shareholders....... because their payout (dividend, distribution, etc...) is being diverted to the lender with absolutely no end in sight.
