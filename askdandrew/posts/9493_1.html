Roger Nelson commented May 13 2013
  • staff

    Roger

    Great question.

    That was structured as a debt fund when Lemke and I were explaining this back then. There are many ways to do this but I will answer your question and then tell you what to do. I know you’re in the middle of a raise for a hard money lender so I’ll tell you how I would suggest you structure this.

    This was simply as any money that is deployed, the investor gets a “flat 8”% and any other points and interest above the 8 would go to the sponsor or fund manager.

    Out of the initial capital contribution, any expenses related to fund set up and legal would be reimbursed to the sponsor or fund manager, but this needs to be disclosed on the PPM and fund docs.

    Knowing that you are putting a raise around a sponsor, I would suggest that you use preferred equity model that we put together in detail during out latest Hard Money Financier course.

    Simply put, this means that all profits and interest are compounded and are distributed at the end of a period while management fees are paid quarterly. Losses and gains are shared equally among all investors (on a pro rata basis).
