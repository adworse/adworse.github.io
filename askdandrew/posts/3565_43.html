David Wilson commented Feb 3 2011
  • staff

    Good question and this is something all members should read and understand.

    The concepts that we discuss in our trainings are meant to give you an orientation and thought process as to how to structure deals.

    Put another way, (and this is going to be a topic that I'm going to go into deeper during tommorrow's regularly scheduled conference call) is that the commercial and mortgage brokers don't add much value to the deals you present. They are the waiter, you are the chef. They order chicken breast, you determine if it's going to be chicken picatta, tarragon chicken, chicken florentine, whatever.

    The same goes for this. You need to sit back and qualify the deal as one that may not be fundable to one that *could* be fundable based on if these circumstances and events occurred.

    Another analogy is that you all know that if a residential property is overleveraged, that means a possible short sale.

    I answered a question earlier today where a "waiter" brought to one of our alumns a deal asking for a 2nd behind an assumable first. Rather than cruxify the alum, I told him to go back and see if he could apply any of the strategies you've learned to make it more of an attractive deal from the funding standpoint (assuming that the numbers look good). This way the alum would learn something though the process and now he would have a thought process and some experience working in these types of scenarios. Then later in the next deal, he would be able to perhaps execute.

    You do not need to worry about coming out of pocket to pay attorneys and other counsel. That is not your responsibility. We have counsel for that and that is *never* anything an intermediary (you) should pay. If anything, you should be paid for providing this type of advisory work to the buyer or seller's representatives.

    What you merely do is offer the scenarios after you prequalify the deal and then say 'based on if you or your sellers does a, b, or c we would be able to do y for you".

    I said it before and I'm really going to beat this dead horse again: Just because someone asks you for something doesn't mean that is what they really need. Your intelectual capital provides a tremendous amount of value and that is how you get equity spreads in deals, possible retainer fees for your consulting, points and other revenue income.

    You could make a good living providing advisory or what we investment bankers call "fairness opinions", or "strategic alternatives" for big upfront retainers.

    What do you think Dandrew does? This is why we purposely shame you into getting the credibility kit because at some point when we teach this in a continuing ed or video series, it will plug into and compliment your already existing business. And you need to have existing credibility to be able to sell these services.

    I hope this answers your question. Its a little long winded, I appreciate that. But I want to make sure you see the whole picture here.

    Sal
