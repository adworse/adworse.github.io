    Molly

    Great question. Sometimes we buy notes, and do workouts with the borrowers.

    Regarding financing the note, sometimes people will come to us to buy their note back at a discount. That is essentially refinancing the note.

    The only real material difference is *who’s in control*: meaning, if we “refinancing”, the operator is strong and is still in control.

    If we buy the note, the operator has issues and we’re looking to get control of the asset via foreclosure or to help the borrower out buy either re-casting the note (new term) or a principal reduction because we bought that at a discount.

My concern is if your business qualifies to operate in New York. Foreign qualifying is simply registering to do business in a state other than the one in which you incorporated or formed your LLC. Does your LLC needs to foreign qualify?
There are many factors used in determining the need to foreign qualify. While different states have different criteria for transacting business, consider the following:
• Does your company have a physical presence in the state?

• Does your company accept orders in the state?
• Does your company have a bank account in the state?
Most of our clients that answer yes to any of these statements have us help them foreign qualify their business in the appropriate states.

Consequences of not foreign qualifying
Since there are additional costs—including initial and ongoing fees from both your state of incorporation and state of qualification—you may wonder if the process is really necessary. But state laws require foreign corporations and LLCs doing business within their borders to foreign qualify, and the consequences of not doing so outweigh the costs:
• You may lose access to that state’s court system.
