One idea for an infographic: Lay out the typical income structure for an operator next to that of a fund manager on a page.

An operator is going to be thinking in terms of an acquisition fee, cash flow and equity splits at the asset level. Typically in very simple terms such as a straight 75/25 split. Whereas a fund manager is going to be thinking about both the asset AND the portfolio level and will include items like management fee, performance hurdles and incentives, etc. I find myself explaining this concept to clients who are attempting to evolve past the purely operator mentality.

Whether they want to be a fund manager themselves or simply want to interface with capital that is accustomed to working with fund managers, it would be helpful to familiarize our clients with the primary differences.

"The source of money for an individual purchase is just as important as the property selected. Institutional money however, does come with some strings attached.
