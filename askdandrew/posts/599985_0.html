One of the best discussions I ever read on how to derive cap rate was published by Ray Alcorn on REIclub.com in 2008. When I first read this (in 2008), it became clear to me how different positions in an investment (in the capital stack) will have different risk and return profiles. It helped me to realize that a broker's usual declaration of cap rate is, as Ray says "...flawed in it's source." His approach made sense to me and I've referred to it often over the years.

Ray also provides the math which may take a beginner a few reads to grasp (it did for me), but once understood, should provide a good foundation as part of future analysis.

Here's the link: http://www.reiclub.com/articles/deriving-cap-rate

    Milton

    Excellent question. Each asset we or anyone else funds is held in a special purpose LLC (“SPE”). This is standard operating procedure.

    Within that LLC is an *Operating Agreement*, which can be best described as a pre-nup. But it explains who gets what shares of distributions and splits.

    Proceeds are sent to a lock-box and the manager of that lock-box will send out the dispersements accordingly to the *waterfall*.

    Does this help?
