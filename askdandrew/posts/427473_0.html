    Here are the pros and cons of this deal:

    PROS

    1. Area vacancy is 9.3%, which is lower than 49.5% of all U.S. neighborhoods (neighborhoodscout.com)

    2. City has created incentives to attract new businesses into the area; so far a new bank and insurance company have opened up in the area creating new jobs. The City has been actively offering tax incentives to attract new businesses and to develop transit-oriented residential and mixed-use projects.

    3. The City's efforts have been paying off as new jobs have been created, and the influx of people moving into this particular neighborhood is high. The percentage of people who were not living in this neighborhood five years ago is higher than 97% of U.S. neighborhoods (neighborhoodscout.com). This fact coupled with a lower vacancy rate suggests a positive outlook for rental demand in this neighborhood.

    4. There are institutional buyers who would buy low income projects, or project-based section 8 properties; so this property could potentially be positioned and sold as such to institutional buyers who specialize in acquiring and operating low income / affordable housing assets.


    CONS

    1. Neighborhood is currently classified as low income; it's income profile shows that it is lower than 85.2% of U.S. neighborhoods (neighborhoodscout.com)

    2. Childhood poverty rate in this neighborhood is 36.4%, which means that 36.4% of children living in this neighborhood are living under the federal poverty line. This is higher than 82.6% of U.S. neighborhoods.

    3. Low income areas tend to have higher crime rates, which translates into higher--and often unpredictable--operating costs.

    Usually, this would be a no-brainer decision for me since I prefer higher income areas, but I cannot ignore the economic lead indicators that say this particular area is about to rise. This is a value play waiting to happen, especially with rents on the rise, as the demand for housing grows with the new jobs being created.

    In your opinion, Sal, do the Pros out-weigh the cons?
