Sal,
I reviewed your reply to my fund formation question but did not see where I could reply, so I wanted to answer the very good questions you posed which I did take into account.

First, the funds monies are always protected because the sponsor operator would not be given a blank check so to speak, the funds would be placed into escrow and held until certain caveats are performed such as end buyer providing their funds to close on the B-C transaction whether the closing date is 24 hours or 30 days from close of escrow for the A-B transaction.

The funds attorney would handle all legal including filing suits if need be and the costs for legal would be born out of the funds operating capital. The losing party would reimburse the fund

The earnest money would be held in escrow until the close of the A-B and the funds monies would not be in jeopardy because it would always be in the closing segments hands until the deal closed. If the deal does not close, all monies advanced by the fund would be returned.
The fund would be given a equity stake in each property or note/note pool until the monies were repaid, this way, in the event extended transaction funds came into play as opposed to 24 hour short term and not repaid, the fund would foreclose on the asset or assets and liquidate to return to the investors their monies.

The mission of the fund is to make available commercial real estate secured short term to medium term investment opportunities where the investors money is always working and generating returns on a daily to monthly basis as opposed to the stock market or hard money lending which ties up an investors money for six months to twelve months gaining interest only monthly payments .

Through my fund, they are receiving one check that reflects profits from multiple closed transactions monthly thus generating a higher return on investment.
