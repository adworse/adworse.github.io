    Satya (Ms Truth!):

    1. *Balance Sheets*
    Great question. Right now, most “Life Companies” (think Snoopy) are providing loans. We’ll make sure the borrower or the asset is ready to refinance with them later on.

    I’m going to discuss this on the next Conference Call if you ask me, but in essence here it goes. The life insurance policies that you and your parents, grandparents have are underwritten by an insurance company. Those premiums aren’t held in a bank account. They are re-invested into cash-flowing assets. This is what pays the death benefit when someone dies.

    2. *Fire In The Movie Theater*
    There are only two doors in commercial real estate for an Exit Strategy: refinance or sell.

    Today, there is a fire in the movie theater and the refinance is locked. So a sale to a REIT is the most viable option. As I stated on #1 above, the refinance door is slightly ajar but not enough. What we have done is refinance the loan to a medium term (5 year) bridge loan through another lender we have relationships with. Life Companies are also getting back into the game and want to speak about this more as they are sitting a lot of cash.

    Does this help you?
