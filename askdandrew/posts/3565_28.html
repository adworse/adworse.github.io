William Clausen commented Nov 7 2013
  • staff

    Great question.

    Yes, when a potential borrower files for Bankruptcy (“BK”), they need to submit plans for how they are going to pay back their creditors. Sometimes this is done ahead of the filing called a “prepack BK” as the creditors know something is going to happen ahead of time and have agreed – in principle only – for who will get how many beans after the asset emerges from BK.

    We are working with one of our intermediaries now on a BK with the sponsor looking to cram down the creditors, both secured and unsecured.

    Obviously, we can’t write a term sheet or make any commitments until the plan is approved by and blessed by a bankruptcy judge. And in my experience, this usually happens quickly at the end of a long process.

    Of course, if you want to know more about this, I urge you to get the course we did a while ago called DIP Financier. (“Debtor-In-Possession”) that talks about this in a Video and Manual Series. We’ve not released it in a while but it’s always a great seller as our Intermediaries are always run into these circumstances now more often than they have before. Its an ebb and flow, sometimes there are a lot of BKs, sometimes there aren’t.

    But as long as humans manage and rent real estate, bankruptcies will always be around, in good and bad markets.
