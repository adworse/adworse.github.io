When I think about my community, I come up with this: People are doing more with less & they seem generally battered, flinching, and unhappy. I live in an area of the country where unemployment is very low - mostly thanks to government jobs and biotech. But when I talk with my friends and neighbors, their mood is subdued. People don't feel like their labor is appreciated. They worry about their retirement and entitlements. They commute to work with heads down and seem to me to be stuck in a paradigm that peeked and is sliding into obsolescence.

When I think about my family, I consider the fact that we made the time to dismantle the old paradigm and forge a new one. As a family that's made a living working for others, we had a good lifestyle. Better than most, in fact. My wife is a medical provider. I was a high ranking federal employee with 20 years of service behind me and a strong track record of leadership and performance. Together, we were easily in the top 1.5% according to this chart: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Affluence_in_the_United_States. In part due to the unique nature of my job in the Federal government, we saw writing on the wall in bold letters and believed we had a difficult decision to make: throw our lot in with a dying paradigm, or embrace a new one.

When I make things personal and focus on myself: I have yet to break even, but I see signs that I was hoping I would see. The decision we made was the right one. I gave up a few years of W2 income (about $500k), paid out a bolus of additional after-tax money (about $150k) for education. In my mind I gave myself the necessary skills to throw judo moves on the economy. Instead of bracing myself for impact, as I would have if I held onto my job (as my neighbors are), I got out of the way. I learned how the financial system works and how I can benefit from it's movements. I learned how to make money when things are bad and when things are good. I'm getting paid for my knowledge - much of it, I acquired by participating as a member in the Dandrew community. This month, I made about twice as much as I did in any given month when I was working for the Federal Government.

My security is no longer in the hands of an employer - who is at the mercy of economic ups and downs. My security comes from the fact that I now know, better than most, how things work and how I can add value to people's lives - no matter what the situation. When things are bad, people need money to make it better. When things are good, people need help protecting their money for the bad times. Everyone needs better yield. There is always opportunity for folks like us. As gravy, we work with an alternative asset class that will always be needed, generates better yield, and offers more security in balance than just about any other asset class.

The purpose of this post is to celebrate with you - the good news that we are among the few who are on a path that leads us away from trouble and towards prosperity. When our entitlement programs fail, we will be okay. We know how to create value that people are willing to pay for. When the dollar pops and flies around like a balloon with a hole in it, we will be okay. The principles we learned here still apply. We can syndicate chickens and toilet paper if need be.

I'm feeling pretty good right now - despite the gloomy news we hear rumbling like thunder in the background. I feel like the people I call are (or should be) happy to hear from me. That's not too shabby.
