    Ed,

    I'm going to put on my Deal Captain hat, and take this question.

    I'm going to assume that the asset class is multifamily, because you use the descriptor "102 Doors".

    I realize that the DC market has historically been, and continues to be a bubble, but for our purposes (because I represent our capital), I'm going to address this as if it is any other market across the USA.

    1) What is their basis? How do we know how good or bad of a deal it is, if we don't know the sponsor's basis?

    2) What's the occupancy? Is this stabilized, distressed, value add? This answer will tell me that.

    2) The BPO (which we only treat as trivial information, because frankly, we just don't trust someone else's BPO or appraisal) puts the value of this property at 6.6% cap. Remember we're in a market top right now. Our advice to investors (as well as is the voice of our own capital) is to keep their powder dry. When a 6.6% cap is considered a good deal, wait for a market change, and then in vest in the better deals.

    3) If we value this at a 10% cap, like we like do as a rule of thumb, that puts the current value of this property at $6.6mm. The sponsor's minimum loan request is higher than 100% of this. That doesn't get us too excited.

    4) Even if we did accept the BPO value as the true value (which we probably won't do), even then, the sponsor is asking for 78-90% of current value. That is too high for bridge, usually. And where does the balance come from? His equity, or is he raising this capital? And then what position will his investors take in this deal? Debt? Equity? It may not be worth your time here to be asking these questions, knowing that it's probably a pass for us, but we want you always to be thinking about these questions, consistently.

    5) What's the ProForma NOI? (cuz we know it's a lie...but it tells us what the sponsor is thinking for value add, if there is any).

    Ed, do you see how I'm putting together pieces to your puzzle?

    Good questions. This is the exact environment where we work to help people think like a bank, and think like a dealmaker.

    Let us know if you get any additional info. I'd be happy to speak to that here.
