Michele Spitz commented Jun 10 2014
  • staff

    Michele

    Great question!!! It follows what is in the Intercreditor Agreement, and usually, even though these loans are sliced behind closed doors, usually what happens is the A/B portion is still wrapped under the umbrella of "debt" in the Intercreditor Agreement.

    Now, *the mezz financing 99.9% of the time is originated at the same time as the first trust debt or senior debt.* So what happens to that debt as far as it being split is usually not consequential to the mezz holder as that happens *after* the loan / cap stack is originated.

    Debt is debt and it has its own set of remedies. So if the A forecloses, so does the B by default.

    Your pal,
