For example, can the 25% come from the seller carrying back 25% second, or can the 25% come from, say,a partner or from a hard money loan, etc. I would like to hear as many acceptable ways as possible so that I will know how to handle this matter with various borrowers who have this matter as a question.

HUBBART LACY Jr commented Oct 31 2013
  • staff

    Hubbart,

    This is a great question. And it is probably best answered during a Continuing Ed. But you’re not going to let me off easy here, so I’ll take a shot here:

    1. The 25% is most likely / will likely be *Preferred Equity*.
    This is the “down payment” money that folks will bring to the table. This can be equity that is raised via Dandrew (if the project is strong enough) or *syndicated*

    2. First Trust Deed debt, or first mortgages never want a 2nd behind them.
    The reason for this is that in most cases, all secured creditors are seen as being “equal”. We don’t allow 2nds behind our loans for this reason.

    3. Many experienced and some not-so-experienced intermediaries are *syndicating equity* around this 25%. This comes from many smaller mom-and-pop investors’ *Self Directed IRAs*.

    4. Lastly, *your sponsor is really telling you they are probably broke and inexperienced if they don’t know that no lender is going to lend 100% of the acquisition price of an asset*. I would strongly suggest you watch the last continuing ed we did that discusses this in detail.

    Your Pal,
    Sal
