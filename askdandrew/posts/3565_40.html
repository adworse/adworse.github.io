Guys and Gals, I always have something to say. I hope it helps. I am of course not Sal, Corey or Peter, but I do have alot of experience. Lately, it seems as if there are questions as to compensation paid on deals. When you are working through a Hedge Fund for a deal, there is no such thing as a vanilla deal, compensation wise. I will say that the fees paid out from DCP are better than fair. For example, if you were doing a commercial acquisition transaction through a mainstream mortgage broker firm or direct lender if you can even find one these days, you would often receive around a half of a point to a point and the point is on the high side. Keep in mind that if the lender or brokerage house was going to charge 3 points, that means that they take most of the upfront points. Also the fact that you can share in the Yield spread with Dandrew on some deals, would not happen through a mainstream funding source. You can also forget about having a opportunity to have any portion of a equity kicker, meaning the back end.

I have learned from experience that those who focus on what they are going to make on deals, begin to enter into what is called "Pre-Judging." If you begin entering that realm, you will pass on good potential deals. If you are new to this business, you need to learn that the key to making a living in this business is to always have a deal flow, Sal and Corey say a minimum of four at any one time, and if you watched the videos, you would have heard that.

The deal flow needs to consist of deals that stand a good chance of closing. Dandrew's and your rate sheet through Dandrew says $2,000,000.00 to $30,000,000.00 plus. I focus on deals $2M to $10M. These are deals that stand a better chance of closing versus deals in the higher price points. Now I have a deal submitted that is $18,900,000.00 and this is not the size I normally focus on, but it is a good deal.

Also if your new, always remember that great deals are created/developed. The majority of deals start out as being a good deal, and once Sal, Corey and Peter begin structuring the deal, it then goes from being a good deal to a great deal.

If you have not watched the videos and understand the concepts or watched the videos and don't understand the concepts, how do you expect to be able to understand the deal (s). Intellectual Capital is what gives you your edge.

From a new Intermediary here to the new gals and guys, focus on finding deals that meet Dandrews standards and let Sal, Corey and Peter work out the compensation part of the deal for you.
