Sal...You used the term " No Promote" can you explain?

Michael Switzer commented Aug 20 2013
  • staff

    Good question. The promote occurs in a Joint Venture deal, usually in a commercial value-added or land development deal where an equity investor comes in and adds what you’d call the “down payment money”, for lack of a better term (and never use that term in commercial, its equity) on top of a debt piece and you split the profits off of some pre-agreed term.

    Usually it’s explained as the equity investor getting a return first, called a preferred coupon, and then the promote is the splits afterwards.

    So, in one of my deals, the investor got a “70 after a 15%”. That means after proceeds came in from a result of a sale or a refinance, the investor got a 15% off the top, and then after that, whatever was left over was split 70/30 with the investor getting the 70%.
