Some who are new may be struggling because they don't really understand what Dandrew is about and what the basis of the system is, so I wanted to give my take.

Sal and the guys are not a group of wealthy guys playing the game of commercial real estate for their own wallets. They are a real firm with two offices. They have overhead, employees, and they are a true Wall Street Investment Firm.

They invest other people's discretionary funds. The clients of the firm come to Dandrew because they know that their funds will be professionally invested and managed. They invest their monies into the various funds that Dandrew manages in house.

Why do these firms go this route? Because what they do is no different than what your own employer does with your 401K. Monies are invested into various funds with these funds chosen because each fund fits the clients investment philosophies. Some funds invest for the short term cash or they invest for the long term wealth, very much like being a real estate investor. You need quick cash, you buy a flip property or if you want long term wealth accumulation, you buy and hold.

The advantage you have in joining the firm as a Intermediary is that your capital provider is Dandrew and you don't have to strike broker deals with multiple firms like many intermediaries have to.

Dandrew makes their money from management fees, equity spreads, joint ventures with other firms, so my point is these guys work very hard. They are serious financiers and they expect each intermediary to be serious as well, this is why they have a credibility kit, serious profit center programs, a trading desk for each intermediary to add to their business.

This is real life, real Wall Street Business and those guys eat, sleep and breath this business and if you listen really well to what Sal says on the Friday calls, he vocalizes this, you just have to listen and hear.

You need to understand that once you enter the Dandrew World, you are expected to operate your intermediary practice as if you are a Wall Street firm. Your expected to work. Your expected to be serious.

Right now you need to ask yourself why your here. What do you want from this? If you are here to become super wealthy and become a jet setter, well than you probably won't see that happen, just because you want it to be. Those who go into a venture with that intention or goal rarely see it happen.

Now instead of waiting for Sal or one of The Dandrew team to get back to you, be pro active and make contact with the client and simply say, "Mr/Mrs/Ms, my funding team is reviewing the initial information you provided to determine if we can fund your deal where it is a win-win for everyone. Now here is something very important to say. "If I were to quote you fees and the such right now, I may be incorrect, however, most deals are 75% of the appraised value, 2 to 3 points, 1 to 3 year term, 10 to 14% interest, with interest only payments monthly, with principle and remaining interest payable at the end of term.

You want to get to know these terms and be able to say them where it does not sound like your reading off a rate sheet. It has to come from you in an authoritive tone of voice.

One more tip. Always control your deal. Have a deal sheet setup for each deal. This is important as it begins a paper trail for each deal. Keep deal specific notes to include dates, times, who you discussed something with.
