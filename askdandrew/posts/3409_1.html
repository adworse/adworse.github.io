  • staff

    Great question!

    A *Loan-Level Bid* means that they want a bid “hung” on each individual loan in the pool. So, if there are 150 loans in a pool,

    Now, this assumes that you’re looking only at loans, and not REOs.


    A more appropriate way to call this is an *“Asset-Level Bid”* which is the all-inclusive term for the kitchen sink of assets you wind up with.

    These are smaller balance rentals and I bet, Molly that these are mostly under 60K. You should be targeting the banks selling these, not investor owners.

    *The way to play this* is the following way:
    1. Tell the owner this isn't for you.

    2. Tell the owner that you can help him sell these off on a bulk flow basis, meaning you would tie up each one of these under contract and sell them off on a pre-determined number per month. Like 10. This is really easy to do.
    2a. If you can negotiate terms on that and/or sell on a contract for deed so if the borrower defaults, you'll hit a home run. The value you bring is making him feel comfortable that you've *structured as much of the risk away from him*.

    3. Sell to your buyers list by running ads on various membership sites or better yet hard money lenders who have borrowers readily available.

    We've done this a few times when Peter was working for us. We may have Matt or some other intern do this for us next summer as we know there is a market for these, unfortunately they are not institutional buyers.
