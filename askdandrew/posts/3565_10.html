  • staff

    Nique

    This is a good question. Some sellers want to see previous track record of occupancy. This is common convention that reinforces conviction in the deal.

    Believe it or not, some sellers will take the best 12 months, or make up some numbers by *averaging* their best months, you name it. Sellers are always liars.

    So, because your pal Sal wants you to look sharp and thoughtful, this is what other *sophisticated investors* are used to seeing.

    This is not the same as the financials, but it is indicative that you, the Intermediary, knows what you are doing and you’re not implicitly insulting your qualified buyers by just flinging poo, and allows you to see how this asset has been managed prior to you shopping out to your qualified buyers.

    They have a convention they are used to seeing. And these models fulfill those obligations pretty well.
    If you’re making between $75,000 and $250,000 (or more) on a trade, this is the least you guys can do.

    Now let me take it a step further. You need to be able to prove value in two ways.

    1. *Prove Your Intellectual Value*
    This means that you’re not just trying to jam a deal across, you’re actually doing some thinking and some of the brain damage *before* sending off to your buyers.

    2. *Prove Value At The Asset-Level*
    This means that you have all the numbers needed. Like what I answered above regarding your question, you’re able to prove this asset has an upside and can show the downside. You’re not just merely hitting the forward button on a sales flyer like someone did over the weekend to me.

    That is how the playmakers make money consistently, steadily and on demand in this business.

    Your Pal,
