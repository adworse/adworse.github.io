    Hurol.

    Thanks again for another Continuing Education idea. But to answer your question now, i would simply say / copy and paste to your sponsors the following:

    *"Our product is a credit facility. Its designed for people like you who have found a portfolio of REOs – that they can acquire at a low basis - to buy, but don’t have all of the capital you need to take it down yourself. Our credit facility would work much like a Home Equity Loan (HELOC) where the interest is paid only on what is taken down.

    When you sell something off, the proceeds of that sale will go to pay down the line the line in a pro-rata amount. This way you can capture sales proceeds and replenish the line.
    Interest payments will be made to us and we will do all servicing.
    Our capital is not cheap. Fortunately for you, we are not a bank. So we’re a low-to-mid teens kind of player. Also, we don’t worry about your personal credit. It’s that basis that you’re buying the asset at that matters most to us.
    Generally, that is how it works. Although we can customize this based on your existing business model, this is usually how they are structured.
    This product is available for REOs only where the title has been cleansed legally. We have different products for non-performing loans and that is more of a JV structure. For now, our biggest product is the Bulk REO Credit Facility.”*

    Let me know if this helps.

    REMEMBER: The lower the basis, the better. If I'm selling you a $100,000 Mercedes for $10,000, that is a good basis.
