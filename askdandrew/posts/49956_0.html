Dear Staff,

While Sal, Corey, and Pearl were commiserating over indications that only 2% of commercial real estate brokers know what we intermediaries do; I had to stop and ask myself if I know.

Fictitious Scenario:

Entity owns a commercial property with a balloon payment due in a year.

Entity has attempted to re-fi with the current lending bank.

Due to same lender having too many loans with this entity – all in the same asset class – the lender has offered a discounted pay off (DPO).

Entity seeks a commercial real estate broker to market, sell, and close on this property within one-year; to cover the DPO.

Property suffers from deferred maintenance, low occupancy rates, and high turnover of tenants.

As an intermediary, I go to the broker and ask if there are any deals they have which we may assist in.

The broker explains the above situation and we meet with the borrower. I make clear that – if the borrower can provide roughly 35% of the DPO as fresh equity – there’s a good chance that we can finance the remaining balance for a couple of years, provide additional funds to bring maintenance current, as well as funds for upgrades like painting, carpeting, and some appliances.

My hope, in doing this, is to enhance the tenants’ living space, reduce tenancy turnover, increase the occupancy rate, and lengthen the available time to sell the property. All of these matters add value, improve the ‘curb appeal’ to potential buyers, and help the broker sell the property.

This means the property may sell for more, which increases the commercial real estate broker’s commission. It also means that broker will be representing a ‘better’ property, which advances their own status amongst their peers and potential listings.

Is this what y’all meant? Am I in the 2%? Out of the ballpark? Somewhere in between?

Sincerely,

Paul Halstrom

    Emisano: Great questions.

    First you’re going to want to use the infographic blueprints that we drop to you on a continual basis as a member. When you got started, you should have received an Owner’s Manual and an infographic called the “Pyramid of Priority”. This will tell you who to contact and in what priority.

    Try to stay away from marketing directly to owners. The reason being is that the Property Managers – as per our Intermediaries’ experience - is much more effective as some of them *actually drive the decision-making* (wealthy owners will just tell them to “handle it” for them).

    Also, in the Private Banker Identity Kit (http://www.PrivateBankerIdentityKit.com) there are 2 great blueprints that show you how to use VA’s or what we call Acquisition Specialists to help you bird dog for these deals. It shows you how to compensate them, hire them, and manage them easily. They don’t need to be in you office, as many of our I’maries’ use virtual assistants to do this.

    Also, I’m releasing an infographic soon for free that will focus on which *Groups on LinkedIn* you should be using. I’m still waiting for my friends on Wall Street to get back to me with their favorite groups to use.

    Title companies can be a great source too. They are on the “first responders” when a deal doesn’t close due to funding.

    Lastly, remember your *Elevator Pitch: “… our company is a capital provider and we fund a lot of things. Our clients are folks who have found something interesting to buy but don’t have all the capital they need, or are folks who have been offered the opportunity to buy their note back at a discount. Those are our main client profiles. “*

    Remember, the best experience you can get is simply by *submitting deals to us*.

    *You need to rest assured that if you find something interesting, we’re here to help you.”

    Sal
