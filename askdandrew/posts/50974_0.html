    Willert,

    I don’t need to tell you that in this business talk is cheap. Everybody “wants to” or “is going to”, but very few actually get anywhere.

    Here is the nuts and bolts of this. And when you speak to these people, picture me in a devil’s outfit over your shoulder prodding you to *ask the hard questions*.

    I can tell you from experience running a $30mm fund that this isn’t as easy as it sounds.

    So here are the hard questions *you will ask them*. After they answer, SHUT UP; *Make them feel awkward with your silence.* Make them ramble on a little. That will tell your gut if you’re working with polyester or silk:

    1. How long have you been looking for capital?
    2. What is your experience working with this asset class?
    3. Tell me what happens first call to funding?
    4. How are you sourcing these deals? What makes you any more special than anyone else.
    5. How big is your infrastructure? Who are your service providers?
    6. What deals have you taken down of this size ask? What was your basis in, and sales price out? Do you have an Excel spreadsheet you can show of recent trades/
    7. How long have you all worked together for?
    8. What is your career background? How long have you been in this space.

    Then, Willert, my friend, I would ask to see a copy of their deck. This is where “Smooth Willert” will come in and say “you’re going to need better answers than that”. Then you commence pitching them consulting with you for you to do their deck for $15,000 along with models. If you get this far, let us know. Then we need to place next steps.
