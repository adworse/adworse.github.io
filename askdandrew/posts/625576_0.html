What I had in mind was buying high yielding hard money residential loans at 10 - 15% apr 65% ltv, then selling an A/B note [ an 'A' piece at 7-8% yield up to 50%ltv (which is 77% of A/B note) and a 'B' piece for the remaining 15% ltv (which is 23% of A/B note) the yield on the 'B' piece would be 16 to 20%.

    Here's an example:
    -SFR home valued at 100k
    -65% ltv first trust deed at 10% apr (broken into an A/B note) yield 6.5k per year
    -A piece up to 50% ltv yield 7% (50k X .07 = 3.5k)
    -B piece capture the remaining 15%ltv as first loss piece (6.5k - 3.5k = 3k) (3k/15k= 20% yield for B piece

    So what I'm interested in is the slicing up part. (is it practical?) I would like to slice a high yielding hard money residential note into 2 to 3 pieces then varying the yield based on ltv exposure. These note will be on SFR homes throughout the U.S. the average loan size will be 100 to 200k. Is this slicing mechanism still done through an A/B note structure?

    This is best sold as what we call a “fractional”. Meaning, one loan, many different owners.

    Hence, the fractionalized trust deed mortgage market.

    So you have a 5 investors at 10K piece for 50K.
