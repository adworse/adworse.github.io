At 28:40 a caller asked why would a bank not sell when they have lots of toxic assets.

it does not matter if a bank has a $trillion in toxic assets if they are not healthy enough to sell those assets they cannot and will not, sometimes they cannot even afford to sell at par. What they will do however is list REO and NPL for sale to temporarily get the FDIC off their backs in order to save their jobs. At best these banks will tell you to go away like they did to the caller at worst they will waste your time with no intention of selling.

What you should look for are banks with high levels of "charge offs" . These are banks that are getting real about selling and have started what is called "fill the files" with data showing upper management and the FDIC why they need to sell their assets at discount. The absolute best and most helpful thing you can offer an asset manager is real word data that they can use to "fill the file" on an asset they are liquidating and in turn reduce its price. If you can supply that info for them you will have a loyal contact that will call you first when selling a new REO or loan.

Hint: The banks health or CAR (capital adequacy ratio) also plays a HUGE role in buying at discount and they will not be able to write down prices if they cannot afford to. On FDIC website look for a banks Teir 1 Capital Ratio which needs to be at or above 4% and a Total Capital Ratio needs to be at or above 8% . These signify the bank is strong, anything less you are wasting your time.
