Carlos Clark commented Sep 13 2014
  • staff

    Carlos:

    Great question.

    May Intermediaries in the past didn’t know what they were doing and we lost some deals in the earlier years when I was starting to teach. They wanted to control the deal and basically they weren’t in control of the deal.

    So what we do is we get direct fast. While speaking to the seller, we also communicate to the Intermediary the range that we’ve agreed upon with the seller in a range and what that may be and how much we’re negotiating your incentives for.

    This way, no one gets hurt and more trades close.

    This isn’t a business like buying homes, there is a lot more room for error if the Intermediary isn’t direct or – worse yet, mis-managing expectations because they don’t want to scare off the buyer… I’ll let you define “mis-managing”…

    Too much money has been left on the table and too many tears from Intermediaries who didn’t know what they were doing.
