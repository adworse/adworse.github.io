    Dan

    First off, welcome aboard! I appreciate the intelligent questions you are asking. They are thoughtful, but I want to make sure there is nothing in your way from getting started and taking action.

    Regarding answers to you questions I’ll answer them in the context you asked:

    *"I want to make sure that I have the ability to, for example: take a location that would be good for a urgent care or possibly a national chain restaurant and do the deal process knowing that you guys will find a home if it works but not in you wheelhouse".*

    Dan, simply put, this isn’t in *anybody’s* wheelhouse that I know of in the institutional side. However, we do tell folks they can raise their own capital from retail (Mom and Pop) investors to get these deals funded. We have one student now that is raising money via a syndication or JV to help an owner / operator of JV of 20 “Red Robin” food franchises buy about a dozen more.

    Regarding Urgent Care (Healthcare), this is the single most radioactive sub-asset class today. I have elaborated on this in the past at length, however, if you look at the challenges both technically, financially and politically with ObamaCare, you’ll know what I’m talking about. Pay for doctors, who are notoriously bad business people are going down and reimbursements aren’t going any higher, perhaps ever.

    *So I would tell you to steer clear of these types of deal scenarios as they probably will never get funded by us or anyone I know.*

    The pay structure is a little different if we do this trade outside of Dandrew or in a “club deal” (where Dandrew funds a percentage of the capital invested, but not 100%) but again, that is on an individual deal basis.

    I hope these answers your questions.

    Sal
