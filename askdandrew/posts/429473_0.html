Hi Sal, is there a minimum population that could be used as a general litmus test; for example a population of 10,000 is clearly too small to provide an adequate tenant-pool, while a city with a population of 50,000 is going to provide a more adequate tenant pool from which to fill vacancies. In other words, the risk of vacancy is much higher with a 200-unit property in a town with a population of only 10,000 people; however, this risk is reduced if we are talking about the same number of units in cities where population is 50,000 or greater. I have spoken with some lenders that will not lend in areas with populations less than 35,000. For the purpose of a general "litmus test," is a market population of 35,000 too small of a market?
