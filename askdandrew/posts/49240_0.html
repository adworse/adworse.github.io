    Salvatore Buscemi commented Mar 3 2015

We have a portfolio of 56 single family rental homes located in South Georgia. The properties have all been renovated, are 100% rented and are producing $480K per year gross income. Based on the tax records and the seller's BPO, the portfolio has a fair market value somewhere between $4M and $4.5M, and loan balances from several different banks totaling $2.5M. The sellers have located a company that will provide funding to refinance the portfolio at 75% of the appraised value up to $3.1M. This new loan would be non-recourse at 5-6% interest amortized over 30 years with a 5 or 10 year call. Monthly payments would be around $16K. Net income after taxes, insurance and note payments will be $100K per year.

Opportunity:
The sellers are tired of managing the properties and are motivated to sell for $3M. The funding providers have agreed to allow the sellers to bring in a managing partner (buyer) during the refinance who will be responsible for the loan. This person will own the majority of the shares of the new LLC in which the properties will be placed in at closing. At closing, the sellers will receive cash from the refinance and afterwards will assign the remaining shares over to the buyer. This process will in essence provide 100% funding for the buyer.

Funding: Sal I know you don't need the funding but I wanted you to se the full offer...

The funding providers are well-known and can be vetted/verified. The buyer must have a credit score of 675 or higher and have a clean background (no history of bankruptcies, foreclosures or felonies). Once the buyer has been approved, the lender will require a deposit of $525 per house to cover their due diligence and appraisal fees. The buyer will be responsible for providing the deposit which will be refundable by the seller in the event that the properties do not appraise high enough to make the trade.

Sal would you consider these?
