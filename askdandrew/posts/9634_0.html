Dave Dejewski commented Jan 27 2015
  • staff

    Dave

    Personally speaking based on my lifetime experience, I *don’t* like the syndicator to be the property manager. It’s a conflict of interest. I think that you many have gotten that Continuing Ed mixed up as the Operator would replace with his own as he had his own accredited Property Management Company. If I recall correctly, that was a larger family office. Not a smaller investor like yourself.

    Property Managers are supposed to manage the property and collect rents. And when you collect the rents, you touch the money. And when you touch the money, interesting things happen.

    Many a deal that we’ve funded has been from a weak, inexperienced Owner/Operator who wanted total control.

    I like my rents to go to a lockbox.

    Putting the Syndicator in the seat of a Property Manager is no different than putting the fox in charge of the hen house.

    No REIT or Limited Partnership I know of would do such a thing. That is just not exercising good fiduciary responsibility or good practice in general.

    And, because you know and love me to be cynical at best, paranoid at worst, there isn’t a single mom-and-pop property manager that doesn’t steal cash. In your more industrial towns with blue collar tenants who pay cash, it’s easy to skim a few off at the top. I’ve told my lie detector story many times. Because it’s true.

    Property Managers follow a protocol, its not as easy as just collecting rents and listening to country music stories. They issue tax statements. Its important work.

    Furthermore, if you’re involved with a syndication with a lot of seminar students – and I’ve seen this happen with a guru who got into a lot of trouble – you’ll see that the student who syndicates wants to wear both hats. That is a desperate move and will always cause trouble. The student syndicator is in California but the property is in Texas. The rents are being sent to the syndicators PO Box *in California*.

    Out of sight, out of mind is what the tenants will think…

    *Lastly, if you’re doing these syndications, and if something goes wrong, you want to be able to point the finger at someone else rather than having that finger pointed at you.*

    Please don’t get into the Property Management business. You’ve spent too much money to not do that.

    Sal
