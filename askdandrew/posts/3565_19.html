Why would someone pay par for a loan pool? Aren't there cases where this actually would work in a buyers favor to do so?

James Balter commented May 21 2014
  • staff

    Ah. Great question.

    *Retail Investors* (aka Ma’s-n-Pa’s) will pay par because they feel as though they are getting a good deal. These are usually the sales pitches from hard money lenders who will sell whole loans or partials to a group of doctors and dentists. The aforementioned doctors and dentists don’t know any better – about seasoning, etc. – and will gladly pay par because they see a face yield of 12-15%.

    Institutional Investors (aka Smarter, More Sophisticated) have higher yield requirements and will pay less for the note to get a higher yield.

    *The lower the price, the higher the yield.*

    *Institutional Investors* also are more sophisticated to buy and bid from banks where the average Retail Investors can’t and won’t.
