What is the difference between a master servicer and a special servicer and what happens once the asset is placed with a special servicer? Also, would we contact the special servicer or master servicer to get more details about the asset? I am assuming this would fall under loans that are packaged and sold as securities. Just looking for clarification.

John Miller commented Dec 5 2014
  • staff

    John

    Good question.

    *Master Servicers* are an entity that acts on behalf of a trustee for security holders' benefit in collecting funds from a borrower, advancing funds in the event of delinquencies and, in the event of default, taking a property through foreclosure. These are the ones that collect and manage loans that are packaged and sold as securities.

    *Special Servicers* deal with those assets that have fallen into default. Upon the occurrence of certain specified events, primarily a default or a bankruptcy, the administration of the loan is transferred to the special servicer. Besides handling defaulted loans, the special servicer also has approval authority over material servicing actions, such as loan assumptions.

    Molly
