    Salvatore Buscemi commented Jul 13 2015

  • Dominique - The "rule of thumb" for maintenance reserves is $250 - $350 per unit per year. This is what lenders want to see. It is usually escrowed along with taxes & insurance reserves. Yes, you can adjust these reserves and it will make the cash flow, NOI and value look better. BUT! There is a real life need for this reserve. You will have capital expenses"CapEx" for replacement items and unforeseen maintenance events that will need to be paid. Remember though, if the property is sold, you get to keep any CapEx in reserve that has not been used. Some buyers want it included in the purchase but I never let em keep it. When I wear my buyer hat...I negotiate it into the purchase. When I wear my seller hat...I never include it in the sale negotiations. Get it? In any case, always, always have a maintenance reserve. I always had an extra CapEx line item built in the proforma. And always need it. That way you, the lender, and any investors are not surprised by needing funds for unforeseen things. In all honesty, if having these reserves makes the deal look less attractive then it probably is and you should pass.
