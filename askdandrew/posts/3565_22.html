Molly Ryan commented Apr 4 2014
  • staff

    Molly

    Great question. Sometimes we buy notes, and do workouts with the borrowers.

    Regarding financing the note, sometimes people will come to us to buy their note back at a discount. That is essentially refinancing the note.

    The only real material difference is *who’s in control*: meaning, if we “refinancing”, the operator is strong and is still in control.

    If we buy the note, the operator has issues and we’re looking to get control of the asset via foreclosure or to help the borrower out buy either re-casting the note (new term) or a principal reduction because we bought that at a discount.

    I hope this answers your question.

I wanted to make a comment about being direct because I think some may not understand what direct means with regard to the buyer.
