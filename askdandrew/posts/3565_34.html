    Salvatore Buscemi commented Jul 11 2012

Sal...can you tell me if there is a difference between a "Credit Facility" and an"Equity Facility"? I know the Credit Facility is essentially Equity, so if it is different....can you please tell me what the difference is? Thanks, Cathy.

Mike Contini commented Jun 27 2012
  • staff

    Yes.

    *Credit:* is exactly what it sounds like. Its debt. Therefore the risk is capped and the upside is capped too. Therefore this risk is that if anything goes wrong, the lender will come in an foreclose on the assets.

    *Equity:* means that there is more risk, remember the Capital Stack "Traffic Light". Now we're in the "red" area where there is more risk, and the control is not there. If anything goes wrong, the equity capital provider will have to step into the shoes of the sponsor/borrower to control the asset. The upside is *not really* capped to compensate for the risk that the capital provider is taking and is therefore structured as a JV most times.
