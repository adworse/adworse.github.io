Jerome Moss commented Oct 21 2017

  • Your on the right track but not totally correct. Charge offs are usually totally written off by the bank. Sometimes buyers like me might see more value in something a bank has completely charged off. For example we bought some receivables of about 100mm from a bank recently that had been written off to zero. I bought those assets from the bank for 2mm dollars a quick pick up for a bank that had them valued at zero. Its really important when you are talking to banks to see what they have and why they have certain assets in certain buckets. Just because it is a bad asset to the bank does not mean it is a bad asset to your customer. I hope that helps and to your success!!
